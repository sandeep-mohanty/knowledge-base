### Simple Diagnostic Routine for Your Scooter’s Motor & Controller

**1. Heat Check**
- After a 10–15 minute ride, touch the motor casing.
- It should feel warm but not uncomfortably hot.  
- If it’s too hot to keep your hand on for more than a few seconds, that’s a warning sign.

**2. Performance Consistency**
- Note acceleration from a stop.  
- Compare with how the scooter felt before the brake drag issue.  
- Any sluggishness or hesitation could indicate motor/controller stress.

**3. Range Tracking**
- Fully charge the battery and ride until low.  
- Compare the distance with your usual range.  
- A significant drop (beyond normal battery aging) may mean the motor is drawing excess current.

**4. Noise & Vibration**
- Listen for unusual humming, grinding, or clicking from the hub.  
- A healthy BLDC motor should be nearly silent except for a faint whir.

**5. Controller Warmth**
- After a ride, check the controller housing (usually near the footboard or under the seat).  
- Warm is normal; hot or smelling of burnt electronics is not.

**6. Freewheel Test**
- Lift the rear wheel and spin it by hand.  
- It should rotate smoothly with minimal resistance.  
- If it feels stiff, the motor bearings may have worn prematurely.

---

### What This Tells You
- **No heat, normal range, smooth acceleration:** Motor and controller are healthy.  
- **Excess heat, reduced range, sluggish response:** Possible motor winding stress or controller strain.  
- **Noise or stiffness:** Mechanical wear inside the hub.

---

### Next Step
If any warning signs appear, a service center can run a **current draw test** and **insulation resistance check** to confirm motor health. But if your scooter feels normal now, chances are the BLDC hub motor survived the brake drag without lasting damage.
